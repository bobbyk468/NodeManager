## Form A — Instructor / Teaching Assistant Consent

**Please read this form carefully before agreeing to participate.**

### What is this study about?

We are researching whether a visual, node-based platform called **NodeGrade** makes it practical for instructors to build and use automated grading pipelines for short-answer and essay questions — without requiring programming knowledge. The grading algorithm at the core of NodeGrade, called *ConceptGrade*, uses a knowledge graph to analyse student responses and estimate grades. This study evaluates how usable the platform is and how closely its grades agree with instructor judgement.

### What will I be asked to do?

Your session will last approximately **75 minutes** and consists of the following phases:

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Briefing | 5 min | Study purpose and consent |
| Onboarding | 5 min | Researcher-guided walkthrough of the interface |
| Task 1 | 15 min | Load a pre-built grading template and make one connection in the graph editor |
| Task 2 | 10 min | Submit a provided sample answer through the student-facing view and interpret the output |
| Task 3 | 10 min | Review two graded outputs and indicate whether you agree or disagree with the grades |
| Buffer | 5 min | Free exploration or overflow from earlier tasks |
| SUS questionnaire | 5 min | Standard 10-item System Usability Scale |
| Semi-structured interview | 15 min | Open-ended conversation about your experience |

After the session, you will also be asked to independently grade a batch of 20 anonymised student answers (10 graded by NodeGrade, 10 by a plain AI model). This takes approximately 20–30 minutes and can be done at a time of your choosing within two weeks of your session.

### Will my session be recorded?

The semi-structured interview will be audio-recorded **only with your explicit permission** (see signature section below). Audio recordings are used solely to produce a transcript for thematic analysis. The transcript will be anonymised before analysis; the audio file will be permanently deleted once the transcript is verified, and no later than 12 months from the date of recording.

Screen activity during the task phases will be recorded by the researcher's screen-capture software to allow accurate measurement of task completion times. These recordings are stored on an encrypted device, accessed only by the research team, and deleted after analysis.

### What data will be collected and how will it be stored?

| Data | How collected | Storage and retention |
|------|--------------|----------------------|
| Task completion times and researcher notes | Observation log | Encrypted spreadsheet; anonymised by participant ID; retained for 5 years |
| SUS questionnaire responses | Paper or online form | Anonymised by participant ID; retained for 5 years |
| Interview transcript | Audio recording → transcription | Encrypted; anonymised; audio deleted after transcript verification; transcript retained for 5 years |
| Grading agreement scores | Researcher comparison spreadsheet | Anonymised; no linkage to name; retained for 5 years |

All data is stored on institution-managed encrypted storage. Only the principal investigator and named research team members have access. Data will not be sold, shared with third parties, or used for any purpose other than this study.

### Will my data be identifiable in publications?

No. Results will be reported in aggregate (e.g., "median SUS score across N instructors"). No individual participant will be named or identifiable in any publication, report, or presentation arising from this research.

### Is participation voluntary?

Yes. Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time during the session without giving a reason and without penalty of any kind. If you withdraw, any data collected up to that point will be deleted unless you indicate otherwise.

If you are a student TA, your participation decision will have no effect on your standing, grades, or relationship with your supervisor.

### Are there any risks?

Risks are minimal. The tasks are non-sensitive and involve no personal questions. You may find some interface elements confusing or frustrating — this is expected and is precisely what the study is designed to measure. There are no physical, psychological, or financial risks.

## Form B — Student Consent

**Please read this form carefully before agreeing to participate.**

### What is this study about?

We are testing how students experience automated feedback from an AI grading system called **NodeGrade**. You will submit one short answer to a course-relevant question; the system will analyse your answer and return a score, a Bloom's taxonomy level, a list of key concepts you covered or missed, and written feedback. We want to understand whether this feedback is clear, fair, and useful.

### What will I be asked to do?

Your session will last approximately **20 minutes**:

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Briefing and consent | 3 min | Study purpose and this consent form |
| Answer submission | 5 min | Write and submit your answer to a prepared question |
| Feedback review | 5 min | Read the score, Bloom's level, missed concepts, and written feedback |
| Survey | 7 min | Complete a short questionnaire about your experience (6 Likert-scale items + one open question) |

### Will my answer or grade be used for my course?

**No.** The answer you submit in this study is for research purposes only. It will not be shared with your course instructor, will not appear in your course record, and will not affect your course grade in any way.

### What data will be collected and how will it be stored?

| Data | How collected | Storage and retention |
|------|--------------|----------------------|
| Your submitted answer and the system-generated grade/feedback | NodeGrade platform export | Pseudonymised (participant ID only — no name, student number, or course linkage); retained for 5 years |
| Survey responses | Online form or paper | Anonymised by participant ID; retained for 5 years |

No data that could directly identify you (name, student ID, email) will be stored alongside your answer or survey responses. The mapping between your participant ID and your identity is held separately, encrypted, and accessible only to the principal investigator. This mapping will be destroyed at the end of the study.

### Will my data appear in publications?

No individual answer or response will be published. Results will be reported in aggregate only (e.g., "mean survey score across N students"). Anonymised excerpts from the open-ended question may appear in qualitative analysis — if so, they will contain no identifying details.

### Is participation voluntary?

Yes. You may decline to participate or withdraw at any time, including after submitting your answer, without any consequence to your course standing or relationship with your instructor or institution.

### Are there any risks?

Risks are minimal. The question is academic in nature and similar to normal coursework. Receiving an automated grade may feel unexpected — please remember this grade is for research only and does not affect your studies. If you find the automated feedback inaccurate or frustrating, this is valuable data for the study and we encourage you to note it in the survey.

### Distributing the forms

- **Instructors:** Provide Form A at the start of the briefing phase. Allow 3–4 minutes for reading. Retain the signed original; provide the participant a photocopy or scanned PDF.
- **Students:** Provide Form B immediately at the start of the student session. If running sessions in a computer lab, an online version (Google Forms with e-signature) is acceptable — attach a PDF copy of the completed form to the participant record.

### Withdrawal procedure

If a participant withdraws during or after the session:
1. Note participant ID, session phase at withdrawal, and date in the researcher log.
2. Delete all data associated with that participant ID from all storage locations within 48 hours.
3. Confirm deletion in writing to the participant if they request it.
4. For the instructor grading batch: if the participant withdraws after submitting graded answers, those answers are excluded from the grading agreement analysis.
