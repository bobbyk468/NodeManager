This chain is the operational definition of H1 (temporal causal attribution).

Figure 4 — Hypotheses H1 & H2 Formal Specification

Pre-Registered Hypotheses · Two Research Questions
H1 — Temporal Causal Attribution
CONTRADICTS interactions cause rubric edits within 30 seconds
Educators who view a CONTRADICTS reasoning step are more likely to edit the rubric within a 30-second window than chance. This is the primary claim linking trace XAI to actionable insight.
H1: P(rubric_edit within 30s | CONTRADICTS interaction) > P(rubric_edit within 30s | baseline) [Condition B > A]
30s PRIMARY 15s sensitivity 60s sensitivity
Analysis: GEE · within_30s ~ condition * trace_gap_count | session_id · per-edit records
H2 — Semantic Concept Alignment
Rubric edits semantically match CONTRADICTS flagged concepts
When educators edit the rubric after a CONTRADICTS interaction, the concept they add/modify is semantically similar to the concept flagged in the trace — above chance alignment. Manual edits only (no Click-to-Add).
H2: P(semantic_match | manual_rubric_edit) > H₀ [hypergeometric null: uniform random selection from rubric of size N, K concepts flagged]
manual edits ONLY CTA reported separately
Analysis: hypergeometric p-value · Levenshtein ≥ 0.80 + domain alias dict · primary = hyper_p_manual
Figure 4. H1 uses a multi-window design (15 s / 30 s / 60 s) pre-registered; 30 s is the primary window. H2 uses the hypergeometric null model split between manual edits (unprompted) and Click-to-Add edits (UI-assisted, always align by construction). Only hyper_p_manual counts for the primary H2 claim.
